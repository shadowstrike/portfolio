Project Overview

Citrix SD-WAN Orchestrator is a cloud-hosted, multi-tenant management service that enables IT administrators and Citrix partners to manage multiple customers with a single pane of glass. The platform provides role-based access control and comprehensive monitoring capabilities for enterprises running SD-WAN infrastructure.

The reporting system was critical for customers to monitor network performance, troubleshoot issues, and make infrastructure decisions. However, the existing report experience had become a major pain point affecting customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.

Current state journey map highlighting customer frustrations

The Problem

Our Product Manager expressed serious concerns about the SD-WAN reporting experience. Customer feedback revealed multiple critical issues:

Inconsistent Report Structures

With tons of features and workflows, multiple reports looked drastically different from one another. No consistent format or navigation patterns across 50+ different reports.

Poor Historical Tracking

Historic views of individual reports were not maintained systematically. Administrators couldn't segregate reports against specific sites, regions, or customers—a critical requirement.

Complex Navigation

Due to complex structure layouts, administrators lost track of which page they were on, leading to delayed or incorrect decisions during problem mitigation.

Severe Performance Issues

HDX report's first-level page took 2 minutes to load. This extreme delay prevented administrators from making quick decisions during critical incidents.

Customer Impact

The portal was very sophisticated as a lot of monitoring and troubleshooting work gets executed and reviewed by customer administrators. When reports failed to provide quick, clear answers, administrators couldn't respond effectively to network issues—directly impacting business operations.

Target Audience

The SD-WAN Orchestrator serves two primary customer segments:

1. Managed Service Providers and Tenants

Customers who consume managed SD-WAN services from Citrix partners, enabled by the multi-tenant SD-WAN Orchestrator service architecture.

2. "Do It Yourself" (DIY) Enterprises

Large enterprises using Citrix SD-WAN Orchestrator as a self-managed service for their own infrastructure. These teams manage SD-WAN deployments across multiple sites, regions, and clouds.

User Needs

  • Network Administrators: Need quick access to real-time monitoring data and historical trends to identify performance issues
  • Operations Teams: Require clear report formats to troubleshoot incidents and make infrastructure decisions under time pressure
  • Engineering Leaders: Need comprehensive visibility across all managed customers and sites from a single interface

User Research & Personas

Through interviews and surveys, we identified three primary personas who interact with SD-WAN reports daily:

Network Administrator - Primary User

Goals:

  • Monitor network performance across multiple sites in real-time
  • Quickly identify and troubleshoot connectivity issues
  • Generate reports for capacity planning and optimization

Pain Points:

  • Reports take 2+ minutes to load during critical incidents
  • Inconsistent report formats cause confusion
  • Cannot filter reports by specific sites or regions
  • No historical data comparison

Key Quote: "When there's a network outage, every second counts. Waiting 2 minutes for a report to load is unacceptable."

Operations Manager - Secondary User

Goals:

  • Oversee multiple customer deployments from single dashboard
  • Review SLA compliance and performance metrics
  • Schedule automated reports for stakeholders

Pain Points:

  • Cannot schedule or automate report delivery
  • No way to export reports in shareable formats
  • Difficult to compare performance across customers
  • Lacks visual data representation (charts/graphs)

Key Quote: "I need to share weekly performance summaries with executives, but there's no easy way to export or schedule reports."

Service Provider Engineer - Power User

Goals:

  • Deep-dive into specific session or site performance
  • Diagnose complex multi-tenant issues
  • Access granular metrics for root cause analysis

Pain Points:

  • Cannot drill down from overview to detailed metrics
  • Navigation structure is confusing and non-intuitive
  • Report layout inconsistency makes troubleshooting harder
  • Missing breadcrumbs or navigation context

Key Quote: "I need to go from high-level overview to specific session details quickly. The current navigation makes me lose track of where I am."

Design Process

Step 1: Research

Working closely with the Product Management Team, I gathered insights through multiple research methods:

  • User Interviews: Spoke with customers who used the product daily to understand pain points
  • Questionnaires: Collected quantitative data on feature usage and satisfaction
  • Surveys: Identified the most important and essential behaviors for users who would help define the problem better
  • Market Study: Analyzed competitor solutions and industry best practices

I shared existing report formats with peers (Designers, PfriEnds, frnds) to check what they liked or disliked about the products.

Key Research Insights

Based on PM inputs, we collected insights and validated few important and essential behaviors from our users who would help us in defining the problem in a better manner. Interviews provided an in-depth understanding of user behaviors and their daily activities.

Design execution plan: 5-step approach from research to delivery

Step 2: Problem Definition

Once the PM shared observations, I reached out and interviewed customers who used the product on a daily basis. This helped me understand user needs and establish a clear problem definition:

Maintain a Reusable Skeleton Report Structure

Build a foundation that can be applied across any reporting page to achieve consistency and reduce cognitive load.

Three Progressive Disclosure Levels

First level view (eagle's eye view), second level (consolidated data view), and third level (drill-down deeper with detailed data visualization).

Suitable Data Visualization

Add data visualization against reports to help users take critical decisions and make needful adjustments within the infrastructure.

Step 3: Problem Exploration

With insights from customers, I shared these with the Product Managers and Engineering team and highlighted the key areas which need immediate improvements and where we could enhance/add new features/functionalities within the existing reports.

Foundation for Future Scalability

I developed a reusable skeleton report structure which could be applied across any reporting page. This provided a lot of relief to our customers. Once the base foundation gets addressed, multiple chain of features could be designed accordingly which will provide relief to our customers.

Whiteboarding & Ideation

Whiteboarding session, original complex structure, and new scalable navigation with progressive disclosure

Step 4: Solution Design

The brainstormed multiple directions to fix the problem and finalized a plan that addressed all critical issues:

  • Generic Report Structure Format: Build a consistent skeleton that works across all report types
  • Download Reports Locally: Enable local export and group sending capabilities
  • Scheduled Automated Reports: Send reports to fixed number of users based on defined patterns
  • Universal Search: Maintain search across all reports for quick data/feature access
  • Improved Table Formats: Present primary and secondary buttons in a different manner across all reports
  • Data Visualization: Add charts and graphs within report pages
  • Proper Titling: Use clear and direct titles for better comprehension
  • Exhaustive Record Details: Provide complete information at each level

Step 5: Design Delivery

Before handing over designs, I shared them with customers and sought feedback. Both visible and invisible changes were tested with A/B testing.

Based on inputs received, I shared all design deliverables to the engineering team and they implemented them accordingly. The engineering team was able to migrate 50+ reports in a span of 1.5 months.

Testing & Validation

Our HDX report A/B test showed loading time of the first level page was reduced from 2min to 30sec. Users were able to make a quick decision to diagnose problems within 1-2min (earlier it was more than 5-10min).

Final Design Solution

New Reusable Report Structure

The redesigned report structure features three progressive disclosure levels, consistent navigation, and integrated data visualization.

HDX Report progressive disclosure: Generic structure → Dashboard (Level 1) → Consolidated views (Level 2) → Detailed drill-downs (Level 3)

Additional report types: QoS with data visualization, alert reporting, and application usage tracking

Key Learnings

1. Systematic Research Drives Better Solutions

Combining multiple research methods (interviews, questionnaires, surveys, market study) provided a comprehensive understanding of the problem space. User interviews were particularly valuable in uncovering hidden pain points that surveys alone would have missed.

2. Consistency is Critical for Complex Systems

In sophisticated enterprise tools with 50+ different reports, establishing a reusable skeleton structure was transformational. Consistency reduced cognitive load and made the entire platform more approachable.

3. Progressive Disclosure Manages Complexity

The three-level information hierarchy (eagle's eye view → consolidated data → detailed drill-down) allowed users to access exactly the level of detail they needed without overwhelming them upfront.

4. Performance is a Feature

Reducing load time from 2 minutes to 30 seconds wasn't just a technical improvement—it fundamentally changed how administrators could use the tool during critical incidents. Speed directly impacted business outcomes.

5. Scalable Design Systems Pay Long-term Dividends

By creating a generic, reusable report structure, we enabled the engineering team to migrate 50+ reports in 1.5 months—work that would have taken far longer with custom designs for each report.
